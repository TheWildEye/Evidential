Part V - Statutory Declaration

I, {{ certifier.username }}, acting in the capacity of {{ certifier.role }} in the Chain of Custody Evidence Management System, being a person responsible for the management of the relevant activities and operation of the computer system used to create, process, and store the above-mentioned electronic record, do hereby certify as follows:

(a) The electronic record described in Part I was produced by a computer that was being used regularly during the relevant period in the lawful course of investigative activities, and the computer was functioning properly at all material times;

(b) The information contained in the electronic record was regularly fed into the computer in the ordinary course of the said activities and derives from information stored or processed by the computer system in the regular course of those activities;

(c) Throughout the material period during which the computer was used for storing or processing the information, the computer was operating properly and, in any case, the information was not adversely affected by any malfunction or improper use of the computer;

(d) The SHA-256 cryptographic hash recorded at the time of submission (Original Hash, Part II) faithfully represents the digital fingerprint of the evidence file as it existed at the moment of registration. Any discrepancy between the Original Hash and the Current Hash indicates post-registration alteration.

This certificate is issued in accordance with: Section 63, Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam, 2023 (BSA 2023) - formerly Section 65B of the Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - and pursuant to the directions of the Hon'ble Supreme Court of India in Arjun Panditrao Khotkar v. Kailash Kushanrao Gorantyal [2020 SCC Online SC 571 / (2020) 7 SCC 1].

The electronic record, together with this certificate and the chain of custody log, is submitted as admissible secondary evidence under Section 63 of the BSA 2023 and constitutes an "electronic record" as defined under Section 2(1)(t) of the Information Technology Act, 2000.

Part VI - Evidence Format & Admissibility Note

Images: JPEG (.jpg / .jpeg) and PNG (.png) - widely accepted as photographic evidence in Indian courts. Digital photographs are admissible as documentary evidence (secondary evidence with this certificate).
Video: MP4 (.mp4 / H.264+AAC), MOV (.mov), and WebM (.webm) - accepted as electronic video evidence. MP4 is the recommended format for court submission due to universal playback support. Live recordings generated by this system in WebM format retain full integrity via SHA-256 hash.
Documents: PDF (.pdf) and plain text (.txt).
Audio: MP3 (.mp3) and WAV (.wav).
